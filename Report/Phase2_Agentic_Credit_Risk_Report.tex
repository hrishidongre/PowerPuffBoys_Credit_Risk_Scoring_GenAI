\section{Abstract}

Phase~2 of the Intelligent Credit Risk Scoring Engine transforms a static ML classifier into a fully agentic AI system. A three-node LangGraph pipeline connects a \textbf{LightGBM + SMOTE} risk classifier (80.41\% accuracy, Macro F1 0.71, 100 features), a ChromaDB RAG module with \textbf{2,931 regulatory chunks} from three official RBI/SEBI documents, and a Groq-hosted \texttt{llama-3.3-70b-versatile} model that synthesizes all signals into a structured, grounded JSON risk report. The full pipeline --- from raw bureau input to explainable credit assessment --- completes in approximately \textbf{10 seconds}.

% ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Introduction}

Credit risk classification answers \textit{how risky} a borrower is. Phase~1 answered that well --- a behavioral ML classifier trained on CIBIL bureau data predicted risk tiers (P1--P4) at 78\% accuracy, deliberately excluding \texttt{Credit\_Score} to learn from raw signals like enquiry frequency, delinquency history, and payment patterns.

Phase~2 answers the harder questions: \textit{Why? What should the bank do? What does regulation say?} It wraps the Phase~1 model in an \textbf{agentic pipeline} that retrieves relevant lending guidelines and produces a document-backed advisory report for credit officers --- not just a label.

% ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Phase 2 Architecture --- The Agentic Layer}

Three sequential LangGraph nodes share a typed state object (\texttt{TypedDict}). Each node has a single responsibility. If a node fails it sets an error flag; downstream nodes detect it and skip, preventing silent failures. The shared state carries: \texttt{prospect\_id}, raw \texttt{features}, the assigned \texttt{risk\_tier}, \texttt{probabilities}, \texttt{top\_risk\_factors}, \texttt{retrieved\_chunks}, the final \texttt{report}, and an \texttt{error} field.

\section{Node 1 --- Risk Analyzer}

\subsection{Preprocessing Pipeline}

The preprocessing exactly mirrors the training pipeline to eliminate train-serve skew. In order: sentinel values ($-99999$) are replaced with \texttt{NaN}; leakage columns (\texttt{Credit\_Score}, \texttt{CC\_utilization}, \texttt{PL\_utilization}) are dropped; delinquency nulls are filled with 0; five composite behavioral features are computed; categorical variables are one-hot encoded (\texttt{drop\_first=True}); and the result is reindexed to exactly the 100 training columns, with any missing column filled with 0.

\subsection{Engineered Features}

\subsection{Model Inference}

The frozen LightGBM model (\texttt{models/finalized\_model.joblib}) is invoked via the low-level \texttt{Booster.predict()} API. Three values are written to shared state: the risk tier (P1--P4 from \texttt{argmax}), class probabilities for all four tiers, and the top 5 features by importance with their live input values.

% ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Node 2 --- Guideline Retriever (RAG)}

The Guideline Retriever builds a natural-language query from the assigned risk tier and top three risk factors, embeds it locally using \texttt{all-MiniLM-L6-v2} (384-dim, no external API call), and searches the ChromaDB vector store via cosine similarity. The \textbf{4 most relevant chunks} are returned with their source document names and distance scores, then passed directly into the Node~3 prompt.

% ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Node 3 --- Report Generator}

\subsection{Prompt Design}

The system prompt constrains the LLM as a bank credit risk analyst with strict grounding rules: use only facts from the retrieved guidelines, respond with valid JSON only, and state ``Insufficient guideline context'' when guidelines do not cover a point. The user message includes the prospect ID, risk tier, model probabilities, top risk factors, and the four retrieved chunks (\texttt{temperature=0.1}, \texttt{max\_tokens=1200}).

\vspace{4pt}
\begin{tcolorbox}[keybox, title={Design Decision: Probability Integrity}]
\small After parsing the LLM response, the \texttt{confidence} block is \textbf{overwritten} with the ML model's actual probabilities. The LLM's self-reported certainty is always discarded --- probability scores must come from the calibrated LightGBM model.
\end{tcolorbox}

\section{Knowledge Base Construction}

\subsection{Source Documents}

\subsection{Chunking and Embedding}

Documents are split with a 500-character sliding window (100-character overlap, minimum 50 characters) producing \textbf{2,931 chunks}. All chunks are embedded offline in a single batch pass using \texttt{all-MiniLM-L6-v2} (\texttt{batch\_size=64}) and stored in ChromaDB with a cosine-distance HNSW index. At query time only the search query is re-embedded; document vectors are retrieved from disk.

\section{LightGBM Upgrade and Model Comparison}

Phase~1 used HistGradientBoosting with cost-sensitive weighting. Phase~2 upgrades to \textbf{LightGBM + SMOTE} to address severe class imbalance (P2 alone is 62.7\% of records). SMOTE synthetically generates minority-class samples before training, producing stronger decision boundaries for P1, P3, and P4.

\section{Conclusion}

Phase~2 delivers a complete agentic credit risk intelligence system. The \textbf{LightGBM + SMOTE} classifier achieves 80.4\% accuracy and drives all downstream reasoning. The \textbf{RAG pipeline} grounds every recommendation in real regulatory content from RBI and SEBI circulars, preventing hallucination. The \textbf{LangGraph orchestration} ties everything together in a fault-tolerant, explainable workflow that a credit officer can act on --- not just a risk label, but a full advisory report with factors, guidelines, and actions, delivered in under 10 seconds.
